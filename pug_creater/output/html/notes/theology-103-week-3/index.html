Week 3: Humans and Sin, Angels and Demons

Since the enlightenment, humans have thought of themselves as inherently "good".

These are the following learning that I was grateful to learn about this week:

  1. Society presents sin as stemming from the personal environment they grew up in. So if you grew up in a rough environment you are likely to be a greater sinner than someone in a good environment.
  2. From a biblical understanding sin is man usurping God's authority and acting as they are autonomist
  3. Both Satan and Adam were unsatisfied with their perfect condition, and the rebelled and desired to be like God
  4. There are three types of death: spiritual death(every unbeliever), physical death(everyone will face unless they are raptured), eternal death(final result for the unbeliever)
  5. Original sin not only includes the first sin that Adam commit, but also it include the state of people who descended from Adam
  6. Transmission of Adam sin seems to be best fit with the representative view. Representative headship asserts that the action of a representative results is seen as the action also for those united to him.
  7. The action of our federal head lead to not only a sinful nature, but also our condemnation, so in the same the action by our new federal head Christ we receive life. (Rom. 5; 1 Cor. 15)
  8. Total depravity does not conclude that man is not able to do good rather it teaches that the corruption of sin pollutes the person. Both body and spirit are corrupted by sin
  9. Man is not relatively neutral in which they are able to accept or reject God. Man is a hater of God who can not understand the things of God
  10. The "sin that leads to death" is a sin that lead to drastic chastisement
  11. Mortal and Venial sins are antithetical to the bible because it supposes that there are some sins that will not lead to condemnation which in turn leads to a faulty salvation
  12. The Roman Catholic idea of meritorious penance which is used for removal of mortal sin is an error and takes a blow at Christ atoning sacrifice for sin
  13. Personal sin does not break the bond between them and Christ, but they do have a negative impact on communion with Christ
  14. God created the world perfect, the reason the world is the way it is, is because man has to face the consequences for their sin
  15. The truth gives eternal perspective to our temporal sufferings in a fallen world
