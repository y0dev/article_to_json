The Trinity

These are the following learning that I was grateful to learn about this week:

  1. The trinity is a doctrine stating that God is absolutely and eternally one essence subsisting in three distinct and ordered persons without any division or replication
  2. The trinity is defined using negative statements which is known as apophatic theology
  3. Division and replication both results in three gods which the Trinity is not stating in their definition
    • This will lead into many heresies hence the reasons of the cults of old and today
    • Each person of the trinity possess equally the full and divine essence of God
  4. Modes of subsistence reveals the personal properties that distinguish each member of the Trinity
    • Father, Son, and Holy Spirit
  5. These relationships establish a definite order within the Trinity, so with respect to relationship ONLY not essence or glory. (1. Father, 2. Son, 3. Holy Spirit)
  6. Psalm 45:6-7 refers to the Messiah as "God" and is enthroned, having been anointed by "God"
  7. New Testament writer identify that Jesus is the Lord that the psalmist is speaking of in Psalm 110:1
    • The Son is both elohim and adonai
    • The Shema in Deuteronomy 6:7 allows for a plurality in God
  8. The word one in the Hebrew "ekhad" affirms God's unity while also allowing for plurality. Gen 2:24 is a example "one" flesh even though there is two people
  9. OT presents the angel of Yahweh as Yahweh and also distinct from Yahweh
    • (Ex. 23:20-23) Yahweh sent the angel of Yahweh
    • Wisdom may be depicted as a distinct entity, but NT writers speak of Christ as "the wisdom of God"
  10. Scripture presents a emphasis on the number three such as the seraphim in (Isa. 6:3)
  11. (Isa. 48:12-16) This passage from the Old Testament presents at least two distinct entities
  12. The early church saw the threefold blessing as an indication as three persons of the Trinity
  13. During the great commission Jesus told His disciples to baptize in the "name" which is singular
    • cf. Mat. 28:19

Sola Scriptura ( Scripture Alone ), Solus Christus ( Christ Alone ), Sola fide ( Faith Alone ), Sola Gratia ( Grace Alone ), and Soli Deo Gloria ( Glory to God Alone )
