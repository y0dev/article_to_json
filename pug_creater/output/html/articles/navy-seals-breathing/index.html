Navy Seals & Breathing

Thursday, October 06, 2022

|

2 mins

HealthFitness

Intro

Ever wonder why Navy SEALs are some of the world's most elite warriors? It's as simple as "breathing" literally. According to the Lung Association of Canada, we take on average 22,000 breaths a day. Wow, we take 22,000 breaths a day and don't give thanks for even a quarter of it.

Thanks God for His mercy to allow us to take these breaths! Psalm 136

We can do a lot to help ourselves by practicing better breathing patterns. There are many different breathing patterns that can result in more energy and a clearer head. The well known breathing pattern is known as "Box Breathing". In this pattern you would exhale and inhale for the same duration around a box so to speak.

  1. Inhale along the left for 4 seconds
  2. Exhale along the top for 4 seconds
  3. Inside the along the right for 4 seconds
  4. Exhale along the bottom for 4 seconds

Box breathing helps you deal effectively with stress and anxiety. It also brings balance to your body and mind along with regulating your natural heart rhythm.

Training

Navy Seals train in such a way to help them control their breathing not only to stay calm, but to also conserve energy. It starts with breathing and proper breathing. Proper breathing begins with breathing through the nose and out the mouth. We heard this before early in our sports career. Little that we know, that is very beneficial to our health. The benefits are:

  • Reduces exposure to foreign substances
  • Increases oxygen uptake and circulation
  • Aids our immune system
  • Slows down breathing
  • Improves lung volume
  • Humidifies inhaled air

Here is a workout or things to do while working out that I believe will help with teaching yourself proper breathing and self control. 100s of pushups, burpees and squats. While at the bottom of a squat just breathe deep inhales, so that you will fill your lungs and slow exhales. The reasoning behind this is because shallow breaths leads to:

  • Increased blood pressure
  • Increased stress response
  • Reduced oxygen intake
  • Impaired thinking
  • Slower recovery

While running, fill your lungs by breathing in through your nose only after each run or after each set. Sharp inhales through the nose and soft exhales through the mouth or nose.

Finally

Start small!

  1. Break up your big goals into small chunks, one breath at a time
  2. Breath control is stress control
